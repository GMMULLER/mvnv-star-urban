This is a companion website for a review article on visual analytics for 3D urban data

Authors: Fabio Miranda, Thomas Ortner, Gustavo Moreira, Maryam Hosseini, Marcos Lage, Filip Biljecki, Claudio T. Silva, Nivan Ferreira

The urbanization and verticalization of cities around the world increase the impact of three-dimensional urban structures in many phenomena of interest to various urban stakeholders. Recently, with the growing availability of 3D urban data, several research contributions have targeted 3D-oriented visual analysis tasks in urban areas. However, the third dimension strengthens the difficulty in addressing the various challenges in designing effective visual analytics tools. In this paper, we present a survey on visual analytics for 3D urban data. Our work characterizes published works along three main dimensions (why, what, and how), considering use cases, analysis tasks, data, visualizations, and interactions. We provide a fine-grained categorization of published works from visualization journals and conferences, as well as a myriad of urban domains, including urban planning, architecture, and engineering. By including the perspectives from both urban and visualization experts, we assess gaps in the literature, motivate visualization researchers to understand challenges and opportunities, and indicate future research directions.

Use our wizard to browse through a corpus of more than 90 papers covering a period of more than ten years and almost 20 venues.

Feel free to get in touch if you have any questions or comments.

Use the Wizard

Use the wizard tab to navigate and filter the surveyed papers. We summarize previous visualization and domain-specific contributions using an interrogative method that categorize the papers concerning three questions:


  • Why is 3D urban data being analyzed
  • What data is being analyzed
  • How it is being analyzed
